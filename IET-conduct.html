Introduction to IET Code of Practice

5th Edition - In-service Inspection and Testing of Electrical Equipment

Version 5.0 | Published: 2020

üìñ Overview

The IET Code of Practice for In-service Inspection and Testing of Electrical Equipment provides essential guidance for the safety of electrical appliances used in workplaces, public spaces, and rented accommodation.

This 5th Edition reflects changes in legislation, technology, and working practices since the previous edition.

üéØ Purpose

The Code aims to:

  • Ensure electrical equipment remains safe throughout its working life
  • Provide a standardized approach to inspection and testing
  • Help dutyholders comply with legal requirements
  • Reduce electrical accidents in the workplace
  • Promote best practice in electrical safety

üë• Who Should Use This Guide

This guide is essential for:

  • PAT Testers - Qualified personnel conducting tests
  • Contractors - Electrical contractors and maintenance staff
  • Facility Managers - Responsible for workplace safety
  • Landlords - For rental property compliance
  • Health & Safety Officers - Ensuring organizational compliance

Legal Requirements & Regulations

UK Legal Framework for PAT Testing

‚öñÔ∏è Primary Legislation

PAT testing is required under several key pieces of legislation:

  • Health and Safety at Work Act 1974 - General duty of care
  • Electricity at Work Regulations 1989 - Main electrical safety regulations
  • Provision and Use of Work Equipment Regulations 1998 (PUWER)
  • Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999
  • Landlord and Tenant Act 1985 (for rental properties)

üìã Duty Holder Responsibilities

Under the Electricity at Work Regulations 1989:

  • Electrical systems must be maintained to prevent danger
  • All electrical equipment must be safe for use
  • Adequate records of maintenance must be kept
  • Regular inspections and testing must be conducted
  • Competent persons must carry out the work

Equipment Classes & Types

Understanding Different Equipment Categories

üîå Class I Equipment

Definition: Equipment with basic insulation and earth connection

Examples: Kettles, toasters, irons, power tools, desktop computers

Required Tests:

  • Earth continuity test
  • Insulation resistance test
  • Visual inspection
  • Earth leakage test (if applicable)

üõ°Ô∏è Class II Equipment

Definition: Double-insulated equipment (no earth connection)

Examples: Hair dryers, power supplies, chargers, lamps

Required Tests:

  • Insulation resistance test
  • Visual inspection
  • Touch current test (if applicable)

üîã Class III Equipment

Definition: Equipment supplied at Safety Extra Low Voltage (SELV)

Examples: Phone chargers, low voltage lamps, battery-powered tools

Required Tests:

  • Visual inspection only
  • Check power supply unit if separate
